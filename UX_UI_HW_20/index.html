Denver Cat Company

Denver Cat Co. is a local non profit, that wanted a redesign of their website. As a Ux/Ui Designer a part of a group we were tasked to market our skills to this company and provide insight into their user experience.

Definition and Ideation

Proto Persona

We wanted to form a deeper understanding of our users' goals, needs, experiences, and behaviors. So, we created 2 personas for to truly empathize with our ideal users. They were based on user interviews and surveys, and we kept updating them throughout the project as we gathered more data. We used these personas whenever we wanted to step out of ourselves and reconsider our initial ideas.

Interview Plan

We Asked Our Potential Users 9 Questions. The goal during these interviews was to collect more qualitative data. With a range of different individuals and information, it was time to analyze.

Affinity Diagram

The insights gained from our user interviews allowed us to categorize into useful categories. We identified pain points, wants, web adoption experience, and pet experience.

The Task and The Solution

The Task:

As Ux/Ui Designers we were tasked to find, contact, and redesign a local Denver, CO non profit company's website. With a personal connections to one of our group members, we reached out to Denver Cat Company to complementary provide insight into their user experience. Luckily, they had posted on Instagram, that they were looking for a new website design.

The Solution:

Our product was designed to align with their owner, Leila's, mission; “Leila's vision was that of a cozy coffee house with mellow music, books, and comfy chairs, an environment where students, remote workers, families, couples, and friends could all feel at home… with the addition of friendly cats to make everything feel even cozier.“

My Role:

During this project the initial homepage design was really my baby and it was extra disappointing to be rejected for a user centered design. It was my idea to make the website look like the store front and use the door and windows as the menu options with CTAs. This was because users shared that they identified the building before the name of the company.

Desktop Prototypes

Initial Design

During the first initial design, we found inspiration creating a website that matched the owner's vision. That vision “was that of a cozy coffee house with mellow music, books, and comfy chairs, an environment where students, remote workers, families, couples, and friends could all feel at home.” Our team's design drew from the Denver Cat Co. store front and allowed users to feel familiar and more at ease with the process of adopting a cat.

Final Design

After meeting with our stakeholder, Leila, she had shared interest in a polished, sophisticated, and slick website design. Unfortunately, she was uninterested in the user insight we had provided and shared that she was restricted to squarespace to create the site itself. Leila was unable to give detailed information due to her busy schedule so our team struggled to convey our design convincingly.
